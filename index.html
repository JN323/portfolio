Steak

History

Steak was rumored to originate in Italy back in the mid 15th century. There are terms such as "steik", "stickna", and "steikja" to represent a big slice of meat. There has been no sources on specifically who made the first steak, but the way they made the first steak would be cutting a muscle from the meat of a cow and then roasting it. Now today there are many varieties of steak such as ribeyes, tomohawks, sirloins, etc.

This will be a method on how to cook a ribeye steak, this will be cooked to a beautiful Medium Rare, which is the most popular way to get your steak cooked.

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak (Found at mostly markets)
  • Oil (The two options below are recommended, you DONT have to use both)
    • Olive
    • Avocado
  • 3 taplespoons of Unsalted Butter
  • Garlic
  • Thyme or Rosemary
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Shallots

The steak above should be a good result for your steak.

Instructions

  1. Get your ribeye steak and put it in the fridge to let it chill and keep fresh. Hold it there for a few hours preferably overnight.
  2. Once put in the fridge for a while, temper the steak for 30 minutes. (Temper means to take out of the fridge)
  3. Rub the steak with oil on all sides of the steak.
  4. Season the steak with salt and pepper all around the steak, make sure to tap the seasoning in the steak so it can be flavored and seasoned on all sides and to make sure it won't fall.
  5. Get a hot pan, put it do medium-high heat, and then proceed to put some olive/avocado oil on the pan.
  6. Once you can see some smoke come out of the oil, then place the EDGE (Fat Cap) of the steak on the pan to sear the fat.
  7. Hold it for about 2 minutes, then drop the steak onto its side in the pan
  8. Once 4 minutes pass on one side of the steak, flip the steak on its other side and sear it for 3 minutes.
  9. Once seared for 3 minutes, rest it outside the pan (preferably a grid) for 5 minutes.
  10. During those 5 minutes,turn off the heat, get your garlic, crush it, and put it on the pan
  11. Also during those 5 minutes get your shallots and 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter into the pan.
  12. Turn the heat back on to low after the butter has been placed
  13. Once the butter foams, which is when bubbles appear, then put the steak back in.
  14. Once put in, begin to baste the steak, which is to take the oil and the sauce from the pan and pour it on the steak, recommended to use a spoon.
  15. Once basted for a while, drop in your thyme/rosemary
  16. Keep basting on both sides until it reaches an internal temperature of 180 degrees. You can use those cooking thermometers to check that
  17. Once hit 180 degrees, rest the steak back to where you did previously and rest it for 8 minutes
  18. Halfway through resting, flip the steak so both sides can be rested. During then, also pour the oil sauce from the pan onto the steak itself.
  19. Once 8 minutes passes, slice up the steak and bon appetit!
